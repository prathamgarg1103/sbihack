SBI Life Smart Wealth is a **unit-linked insurance plan (ULIP)** — an
insurance-cum-investment product. Diya **never recommends ULIPs** (suitability
rule 1). This fact-sheet exists only so Diya can honestly *flag* a ULIP a user
already holds and ground the exit-vs-stay math.

Charges (year 1): a premium-allocation charge of about **6%** is deducted before
any money is invested; ongoing policy-administration and fund-management charges
total roughly **2.5% per year** of fund value. Returns are market-linked and not
guaranteed.

Exit routes: a **30-day free-look period** from policy receipt allows a full
refund minus proportionate risk charges. After free-look, surrendering during
the **5-year lock-in** moves the fund (minus a discontinuance charge — capped
per IRDAI at about **₹3,000** for this premium band in year 1) into a
discontinued-policy fund earning about **4% p.a.**, payable only after the
lock-in ends. Life cover ceases on surrender.

Honest note: staying can make sense if premiums are comfortably affordable —
most charges are front-loaded, so the drag falls in later years and the life
cover continues. It is rarely suitable when the premium is a large share of a
low or fixed income.
