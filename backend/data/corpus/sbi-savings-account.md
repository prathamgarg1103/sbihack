A regular SBI savings account earns 2.70% p.a., calculated on daily balance and
credited quarterly. This is the baseline against which idle-cash options like a
Sweep-FD are compared. Money kept here is fully liquid but earns the least.

Do-nothing baseline: with consumer inflation running at roughly 6% p.a., money
parked at 2.70% loses about 3.3% of purchasing power every year — doing nothing
is itself a choice with a cost. The honest counterweight: doing nothing keeps
the money fully liquid, which has real value for an emergency buffer.
